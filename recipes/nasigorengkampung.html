Nasi Goreng Kampung

Description

Nasi goreng kampung (literally: village fried rice) is a variety of the Malaysian dish nasi goreng, or fried rice. This recipe is adapted from this page.

Ingredients

  • Onion
  • Shallot
  • Chili
  • Dried anchovies
  • Cooking oil
  • Rice
  • Eggs
  • Salt
  • Long beans
  • Water convolvulus (Kangkung)

Steps

  1. Puree the onion, shallot, chili and dried anchovies until it is fine.
  2. Heat the wok on medium heat. Put in oil and fried anchovies until golden, drain and dish up.
  3. With remaining oil in wok, put in the previously pureed ingredients and 1 tbsp of salt and carefully fry until it turns golden.
  4. Put in the rice and turn the heat on high. Toss until it looks even, push the rice aside
  5. Crack your eggs and put it in the center. Spread the eggs around. Immediately push the rice onto the eggs and toss the rice, making sure the eggs at the bottom are all brought up and broken up. Fry for a while.
  6. Put long beans into rice and toss for 15 seconds, followed by water convolvulus. Toss again for 5 seconds, not too long. Dish up and serve.
